Krishna teaches that one should perform their duty without attachment to the results. Selfless action leads to peace and freedom.

The soul is eternal and indestructible. It is not born and does not die. The body may perish, but the soul continues forever.

A wise person remains calm in both happiness and sorrow. They are not disturbed by success or failure.

Desire leads to attachment, and attachment leads to anger. Anger causes confusion and loss of wisdom.

One should follow their own duty even if it is imperfect, rather than following someone else's duty perfectly.

The mind is restless and difficult to control, but it can be trained through practice and detachment.

Meditation helps control the mind and leads to inner peace and clarity.

A true yogi sees all beings equally and treats everyone with compassion and balance.

Knowledge and action must go together. Knowledge without action is useless, and action without knowledge is blind.

Detachment means acting without being affected by results, not avoiding action.

The senses can distract the mind. Controlling the senses leads to self-mastery.

Ego creates illusion and attachment. Letting go of ego leads to wisdom and truth.

Faith and discipline are essential for growth. A person becomes what they believe.

A disciplined person remains steady in all situations and is not controlled by emotions.

Self-realization is the ultimate goal of life. Understanding the true self leads to liberation.

The body is temporary, but the self is permanent. One should not grieve for what is temporary.

A controlled mind becomes a friend, while an uncontrolled mind becomes an enemy.

Actions performed with selfish intent create bondage. Selfless actions bring freedom.

Wisdom comes from reflection, experience, and inner awareness.

A wise person is not affected by external situations and remains stable.

Attachment leads to fear and suffering. Freedom comes from letting go.

Devotion means surrendering to the divine with faith and trust.

A devotee who remembers the divine constantly experiences peace and protection.

Human behavior is influenced by three qualities: purity, activity, and ignorance.

Purity leads to wisdom, activity leads to restlessness, and ignorance leads to confusion.

One must rise above these qualities to achieve true freedom.

Food, thoughts, and actions influence a person's mind and behavior.

Pure actions and thoughts lead to higher awareness.

A balanced life includes discipline in eating, sleeping, working, and relaxation.

A person free from ego, hatred, and attachment is close to the divine.

Forgiveness, humility, and compassion are signs of true wisdom.

The ultimate truth exists beyond the material world and is realized through awareness.

The divine exists in all beings and throughout the universe.

Seeing unity in diversity is true knowledge.

Fear arises from ignorance and attachment. Knowledge removes fear.

A wise person understands life beyond birth and death.

Life continues in cycles until one achieves liberation.

Liberation comes from freedom from desire, ego, and attachment.

A person who offers all actions to the divine remains free from karma.

Karma is the law of action and consequence. Every action produces a result.

Responsible and conscious actions lead to better outcomes.

The purpose of life is to understand one's higher self and achieve inner peace.

True happiness comes from within, not from external objects.

A person who controls desires and emotions lives peacefully.

Courage and righteousness are important in difficult situations.

Doubt weakens the mind, while knowledge strengthens it.

The path to truth requires patience, discipline, and consistency.

A wise person speaks less and understands more.

Silence and self-reflection lead to deeper understanding.

Spiritual growth is gradual and requires effort and consistency.

Balancing knowledge, action, and devotion leads to a complete life.

Freedom from jealousy and anger leads to peace.

The highest state is realizing unity with the divine.

Living with awareness, discipline, and purpose leads to fulfillment.
